Peer pressure



What is peer pressure?


Definition

Peer pressure is when you can get encouraged to change your mind, opinions or actions by other people.
Peer presure can make you do risky things, smoke, change the way you dress or make less effort at school.


Where can peer pressure occur?

Peer pressure can occur in many environments, such as

  • The schoolyard
  • A place where you meet with your friends
  • Even the workplace!

Types of peer pressure


Spoken pressure

When somebody physically askes you to do something that makes you do something that you might not want to do.
This is when someone...

  • Tells you that there will be an end to their friendship if they won't join in
  • Tries to persuade you to do something that you don't want to do
  • Is rude to you so that you will do something

Unspoken pressure

Unspoken pressure is when nobody actually says anything other are doing things and you think that you should too.
This might happen when...

  • Someone popular does something and so you do it too
  • People make their thinking obvious

Standing up to peer pressure


  • Stand up for yourself

    It is Easier to stand up to peer presure if you stand confidently, hold your head high and look the person in the eye. This can have good impact with what you say.

  • Think ahead

    It is good to plan ahead about what you might have to do when you face this type of situation. It is a lot better than doing it on the spot.

  • Say no

    If you don't have the confidence to say something, explain to them in a calm way that you don't want to do it. This should direct their attention to you.
